About The East New York Times

"East New York deserves journalism that treats it like what it is — a living, breathing community full of people making decisions, raising families, and fighting for a better neighborhood."

Who We Are

The East New York Times is an independent community news publication dedicated to covering civic life, local government, and neighborhood stories in East New York, Brooklyn. We are a publication of Urban Version Media.

We cover Community Board 5 meetings, local elections, housing and development, public safety, education, and the everyday stories that shape life in one of Brooklyn's most historic neighborhoods.

Our Mission

East New York has long been underrepresented in mainstream media. Stories that matter to our community — zoning decisions, school board votes, local business closures, housing displacement — rarely make the front page. We're changing that.

We believe that an informed community is an empowered community. Our goal is simple: give East New York the journalism it deserves.

Our Values

Independence

We answer to our community — not advertisers, corporations, or political interests. Our editorial decisions are made independently.

Accountability

We cover the people and institutions that hold power in East New York and hold them accountable to the community they serve.

Community First

Every story we publish starts with the question: how does this affect the people of East New York?

Accessibility

Our journalism is free and available to everyone. Knowledge about your community should never sit behind a paywall.
